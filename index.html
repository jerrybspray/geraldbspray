Twister (1989) Review

  • Release date: June 30, 1989
  • Production company: Vestron Pictures
  • Distribution company: Vestron Pictures
  • Starring: Harry Dean Stanton, Suzy Amis, Crispin Glover, Dylan McDermott
  • Director: Michael Almereyda
  • MPAA rating: PG-13
  • Runtime: 93 minutes
  • Aspect ratio: 1.85:1
  • Sound mix: Dolby Stereo
  • Home video formats: VHS, LaserDisc, DVD

When people talk about "Twister", they're usually referring to the 1996 film, and there's a good reason for that. This 1989 film tries to be both a comedy and a drama, and succeeds at neither. Even though the title is "Twister", the movie is less about a tornado, and more a series of events happening during tornado season. These events surround the Cleveland family, who live in a mansion in Kansas.

I have a confession to make. I couldn't keep track of all the family drama, because I didn't care. The characters don't feel like real human beings, making any attempt at humor feel contrived. At times, I felt like I was watching a drama with comedy elements thrown in just for the sake of having them.

My advice? Stick with the 1996 blockbuster.

Grade: D
