Twister (1989) Review

  • Release date: June 30, 1989
  • Production company: Vestron Pictures
  • Distribution company: Vestron Pictures
  • Starring: Harry Dean Stanton, Suzy Amis, Crispin Glover, Dylan McDermott
  • Director: Michael Almereyda
  • MPAA rating: PG-13
  • Runtime: 93 minutes
  • Aspect ratio: 1.85:1
  • Sound mix: Dolby Stereo
  • Home video formats: VHS, LaserDisc, DVD

When people talk about "Twister", they're usually referring to the 1996 film, and there's a good reason for that. This 1989 film tries to be both a comedy and a drama, and succeeds at neither. Even though the title is "Twister", the movie is less about a tornado, and more a series of events happening during tornado season. These events surround the eccentric Cleveland family, who live in a mansion in Kansas. There's a retired soft drink tycoon, who uses his proceeds to support his daughter Maureen. She wants to raise her child elsewhere, away from kooky characters such as Howdy, an absurdist art student. Meanwhile, her ex wants to get back together with her.

I have a confession to make. I used Wikipedia to help me with that summary, because I just wasn't invested enough to keep track of all the family drama. Maureen doesn't feel like a real human being, instead feeling cut and pasted from a TV soap opera. Characters such as Howdy add some comic relief, but it feels added in just for the sake of having it.

I'm surprised that high-profile actors such as Harry Dean Stanton were convinced to be in this movie. My advice? Stick with the 1996 blockbuster.

Grade: D
